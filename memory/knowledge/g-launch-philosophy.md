# G's Launch Philosophy

**Updated:** Mar 12, 2026

---

## Core Principle

**Ship first, hype second.** G builds products, not content. The product is the message.

---

## Approach

### What Works
- **Shipping speed** — 80% done beats 100% planned
- **Let the product speak** — Good work gets discovered. Bad work doesn't get saved by marketing
- **One post per release** — Tweet/LinkedIn when it ships. That's it. No daily grinding
- **Link back to substance** — Every social post drives to: the app, the portfolio, or a blog post with thinking

### What Doesn't Work (For G)
- Daily tweeting
- Growth hacking tactics
- Pre-launch hype with no product
- Landing pages for unreleased apps
- Treating social media as a job

---

## Why This Works for G

G is not a content creator. He's a builder. His credibility comes from:
1. Products that exist and work
2. Clean documentation
3. Public code (GitHub)
4. Smart design decisions
5. Willingness to ship scrappy and iterate

These things attract the *right* audience: other builders, designers, people who respect craftsmanship. Not followers.

---

## Practical Launch Flow

### Before Ship
- No landing page (waste of energy)
- No countdown timer
- No "coming soon" buzz
- Just: finish the code

### At Ship
1. **Product is live** (marketplace, GitHub, or website)
2. **One tweet:** "Just shipped X. It does Y. [link]"
3. **One LinkedIn post:** Same thing, slightly more formal
4. **That's it.** No follow-up posts the next day

### After Ship
- Ship the next thing
- If you write about it (Medium, blog), post that
- Keep the feed authentic and rare (meaning each post has substance)

## Social Media Reality

**X (@MrG_Ghayyath):** G hates the grind. Super lazy about it. Prefers building to posting.
- **What to do:** 1 tweet per ship. Be authentic. Don't fake enthusiasm.
- **What not to do:** Daily threads, engagement bait, reply guys

**LinkedIn:** More formal network. Job people hang out here.
- **What to do:** 1 post per ship. Mention the craft/thinking if relevant. Keep it short.
- **What not to do:** Corporate speak, overuse of hashtags

**Rule:** If you don't want to write it, don't post it.

---

## Key Decision: Pre-Launch Landing Pages

**Decision:** No standalone landing pages for unreleased apps.

**Why:**
1. **Marketplace discovery** > SEO landing page
   - Figma users find plugins in Figma marketplace, not Google
   - Desktop app users find via Product Hunt / GitHub, not fancy landing page
2. **Energy cost** > benefit
   - Building a landing page takes 2-4 hours
   - Results in ~5 email signups before launch
   - Energy should go to finishing the code
3. **Post-launch:** Subdomain redirects to the actual product (marketplace, GitHub, website)
   - Simple. No bloat. Functional.

---

## What Changes This

Only if:
- **Investor money** (need landing page for pitch deck)
- **Strategic partnership** (need landing page to showcase to partner)
- **Very long timeline** (6+ months, then okay to build hype)

None of these apply to G right now.

---

## The Why Behind This

G values **time** more than **hype**. He knows his own psychology:
- Building energizes him
- Social media drains him
- Shipping gives him dopamine
- Tweeting does not

Working *with* his nature, not against it, is the meta-skill.
