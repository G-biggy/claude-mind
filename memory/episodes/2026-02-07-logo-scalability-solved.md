# Logo Scalability Crisis — Solved with 3-Variant System

**Date:** 2026-02-07
**Significance:** Resolved the "looks great big, unreadable small" problem that was blocking brand asset deployment.

## The Problem

G's pirate-chef logo looked amazing at large sizes but became cluttered and unrecognizable at favicon (16px) and app icon (24-32px) sizes. Thin lines, small gaps, and intricate shapes collapsed.

## The Constraint

G insisted on keeping the Captain Hook / pirate-chef concept. "The whole idea is to play around the 'captain hook' concept, I want to keep that." So simplification had to preserve the pirate DNA.

## What We Explored

I generated 6 SVG concept directions: simplified chef hat, caption bubble + fork, bold C with steam, CC monogram, cooking pot, and a clean hook-C. Also explored a Jolly Roger pattern (skull + crossbones simplified to circle + X) that reads "pirate" even at 16px.

## The Solution: 3-Variant Logo System

Instead of forcing one logo to work everywhere, we created three purpose-built variants:

1. **Horizontal** — Full detail. Icon + "Caption Cook" text side by side. Key alignment: hat to cap-height, mustache to baseline.
2. **Stacked** — Icon above text. For centered/square layouts.
3. **Icon only** — Simplified pirate-chef mark. Survives at 16px.

Font for logotype: **Fredoka** — rounded, friendly, doesn't compete with the character illustration.
