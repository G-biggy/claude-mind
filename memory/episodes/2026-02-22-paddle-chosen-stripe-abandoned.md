# Paddle Chosen — Stripe Atlas Abandoned

**Date:** 2026-02-22
**Significance:** Final decision: Paddle over Stripe Atlas. Lemon Squeezy also evaluated and rejected. Outreach email to Paddle sales team drafted to unlock custom pricing.

## Why Paddle Won Over Lemon Squeezy

Both are Merchant of Record platforms. Key differentiators:
- Lemon Squeezy was acquired by Stripe in July 2024 — future roadmap uncertain
- Paddle is independent, mature, built specifically for SaaS subscriptions
- Paddle handles chargebacks completely (MoR obligation)
- Paddle supports individual sellers without company registration

## The Sub-$10 Pricing Issue

Paddle has a "custom pricing" requirement for products under $10/month. G's Caption Cook pricing: monthly $2.99/$3.99. This technically falls under the threshold.

**G's key distinction:** Caption Cook is NOT a sub-$10 product. It's a full SaaS with blended revenue across monthly ($2.99-3.99), yearly ($19.99-29.99), and lifetime ($39.99-59.99) tiers. Only the lowest monthly tier falls below $10 per transaction.

## Outreach Email to Paddle Sales

Drafted an email to Paddle's sales team emphasizing:
- Caption Cook is a real SaaS product, not a low-value micro-app
- Blended revenue model (multiple tiers, yearly and lifetime are well above $10)
- Goal: get approved for standard pricing without "low-value" restrictions

G wrote his own version of the email. Sage's feedback: remove an awkward Stripe comparison that could come across negatively toward a potential business partner.

## Pre-Launch Checklist

This decision means the Stripe integration that was deeply built into Caption Cook (checkout, webhooks, customer portal, dynamic pricing with coupons) will be rebuilt for Paddle. Launch timeline extended accordingly.
