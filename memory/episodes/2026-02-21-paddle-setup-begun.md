# Paddle Setup Begun — MoR Model Understood

**Date:** 2026-02-21
**Significance:** G started filling out Paddle's onboarding form and had questions about business name, entity type, and whether to wait for the LLC first.

## The Business Name Question

G had previously billed work under "G's Design House" (a spontaneous decision) and worried customers might be unhappy seeing it on their credit card statements.

Resolution: Paddle as Merchant of Record means customers see **"Paddle.com"** on their statements — not G's business name at all. The business name field on Paddle's form is for their internal KYC only.

## Form Guidance Given

- **Business name:** Use "Caption Cook" or a broader umbrella name (for internal KYC only)
- **Business type:** Select "Individual" (no entity registered yet; can update to "Company" after LLC formed)
- **Annual revenue:** "Not yet live" (Caption Cook hadn't launched)
- **Website:** Leave blank or use Caption Cook domain if available

## Strategic Question Raised

Was G setting up Paddle *instead of* or *in parallel with* Stripe Atlas? Paddle's stricter onboarding process might make it worth waiting for LLC details to avoid redoing KYC.

G's implicit answer: Paddle is replacing Stripe Atlas, not running alongside it.

## Key Insight Solidified

Paddle's MoR model eliminates:
- Need for US LLC formation immediately
- Registered agent, annual filings, Delaware franchise tax
- Business name exposure on customer statements
- Complex Stripe integration refactor (new integration needed, but simpler for international sellers)

## Projects Covered by Paddle Account

G planned to use one Paddle account for both Caption Cook and future products (same pattern as original Stripe solo-founder setup).
